Never Eat Alone

I hate networking. I hate the entire thought of meeting people exclusively to grow in a business sense. Never eat alone is an anti-networking book. It's about bulding meaningful relationships and creating something with them. The only networking book you'll need.


  1. Community is the most valuable form of social capital: spurs collaboration while satisfying a deeply human need
  2. Success=People you meet+What you create together
  3. Everything exists solely in relationship to everything else.
  4. Poverty isn’t solely a lack of resources, but also isolation from the people who can help you out of poverty
  5. Key to networking is generosity. Don’t keep score- reciprocity will work its magic. Find ways to make others successful. This builds trust, vitally important for any relationship.
  6. You also need to be able to ask for generosity. Giving help is only half the equation. If you can’t ask for it when you need it, you’re hung out to dry.
  7. The more specific you are about what you want, the easier it is to accomplish. "A goal is a dream with a deadline."
  8. Blue Flame: A convergence of mission and passion, founded on a realistic self-assessment of your abilities.
  9. Steps to find your blue flame:
    1. Write down dream (no matter how preposterous) then write down things which bring you joy and pleasure.
    2. Ask close friends for your greatest strengths and weaknesses.
    3. List what you’d like to accomplish in 3 years. Work back in 1 year and 3 month increments to realize action plans.
    4. Name people who can help get you closer to your goal.
    5. Find a way to be generous to every person you reach out to.
  10. Goals must be specific. They must be challenging and demanding.
  11. Current ways to get involved:
    1. Create a company approved project working to grow skills and meet others in your company.
    2. Take on leadership positions in the hobbies and organizations that interest you.
    3. Join your alumni club
  12. The law of probability ensures the more new people you know, the more opportunities will come your way. 
  13. It never hurts to ask. Breeds opportunity. A low propensity for risk correlates to a low propensity for success. "There is genius, even kindness, in being bold."
  14. Initiate with new people on a weekly basis. 
  15. In reaching out: state the situation, communicate your feelings, deliver the bottom line, end with an open ended question. 
  16. It's better to spend more time with fewer people. Meaningful dialogues matter. Be transparent. 
  17. Don't network, make friends. 
  18. Prepare one page synopsis on someone you're going to meet. Aim to know what they're like, where their passions are. Understand how to build a meaningful relationship with them
  19. Networking goals: map out most important players, determine influencers, make a list of potential customers/acquirers. Name actual people. 
  20. Make every call warm. You must persist to get in touch:
    1. state connection
    2. state value prop.
    3. impart urgency to your meeting.
    4. be prepared to compromise.
    5. Always have a call to action.  
  21. Credibility translates to trust. 
  22. Talk a little and say a lot. 
  23. Make gatekeepers allies. 
  24. Group meetings together to meet more people, add value. 
  25. Meet people around your passions. Friendships are created around quality of time. You can also tell more about how someone will react in business from a workout or dinner.
  26. **Activities to Build Over: Coffee, conferences, workouts/hobbies, meal or drinks, Special Events (make it interesting and fun), Dinner Parties/Your Home, Volunteer together

Conferences
  1. Follow-Up: First is generally best. W/i 12-24 hours. Include a reference to convo, reaffirm commitments, be brief. Handwritten notes go a long way.
  2. **Conference Networking:
    • Offer to help the organizer
      Offer to speak
    • ask questions (introduce yourself, company, and ask a deep one)
    • organize your own dinners/invite people to your home
    • speak to big players(before speaking)
    • Research and share information
    • Master the deep bump.
  3. Deep Bump: brief encounter where you deeply connect, ask questions beyond business, and reveal vulnerability.
  4. Merge your network with other connectors, co-host events. Must be a win-win.
  5. Small Talk tips:
    • Be yourself
    • DON’T make small talk
    • relax, nod your head and lean in, smile, maintain a healthy degree of eye contact
    • Learn to Touch: Shake with two hands, touch the others elbow/upper arm.
    • Have something to say
  6. Johari Window: Succesful communication depends on our ability to align how open we are with how open the others are/expect. Be a Mirror.
  7. In Exiting: Affirm your desire to follow-up, politely express desire to meet more people (see book for example). ALWAYS REMEMBER NAMES.
  8. Discover someone’s motivations.  Generally: Making money, finding love, or changing the world. Every person is an opportunity to help and be helped. Make them feel important.
  9. Biggest impact: Health, Wealth, and Children. Helps fulfill basic needs and move up the hierarchy.
  10. Success comes to those who give freely but also maintain self-interest. They give to givers, feed their network, and calendar time for giving. Look for quick favors that are high-impact. Don’t wait to be asked to give, just give.
  11. Strength of network= quality+quantity+diversity. Who you know determines how effectively you can apply what you know. 
  12. In building new relationships:
    • People should see you in three modes
    • contact at least once a month
    • Two face-to-face meetings for a friend
    • Secondary relationships require 2-3 contacts a year.
  13. Call on Birthdays: POWER-PING. This means more than you can understand. I’ve tested this, and it’s true. Cal your friends on their birthdays. 
  14. Find individuals with access to higher networks that are also accessible: Mentors, bosses, teachers, etc. Anyone who can add electricity. Thursdays are great for dinner parties.
  15. Dinner Parties:
    • Thursdays are great
    • have a theme
    • one big dish and LOTS of alcohol (wine)
    • create atmosphere.
    • Simple is better.
    • Host a virtual after-party (e-mail pics and party highlights).

Internet
  1. On digital media: When you reach out, highlight your interest by showing you’ve read their profile.
  2. Trust=generosity + vulnerability + accountability + candor
  3. Subject lines should imply utility or curiosity. Think, how can I helpful to people? Create value.

  1. Oftentimes we do’t even know what to ask. Engineer time for chance and creativity: create a broad network, plant yourself at a geographic center, become a leader in philanthropy.
  2. Passion generates energy. Stoke the fire with novelty, new things. “If you plan your whole life, you can’t get lucky” 
  3. Focus less on “to-dos” than “to-meets"
  4. Get in front of key influencers. Come to newspeople with interesting stories, not just self-promotional. Always thank people for their work.
  5. Go for mice all day and a lion will still starve. Treat famous people like people, don’t pester them. 
  6. Political fundraisers, associations, and non-profits to meet people.
  7. You learn in your twenties and earn in your thirties.
  8. Bring people together to make the world a better place. Be the best in the world to be the best for the world.
